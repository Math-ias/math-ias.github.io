Dropping Balls

I did some googling here. There’s a rich history behind New Year’s ball drop. It starts in 1903.

The New York Times is opening new headquarters. The newspaper wants something better than fireworks. For inspiration they go down the Hudson river. They look across the Atlantic. And they finish up the Thames. There the Greenwich Royal Observatory timekeepers have been dropping balls since 1833. And they do it daily! And they still do it!

So why drop a ball? Well we have to think like people in the 1800s. You’re stuck with mechanical time keepers. These watches don’t even connect to the internet. And you need to reset your watch. The sun’s position tells you the day is about half done. If you hustle to your favorite observatory then the ball rising up tells you to get ready. The ball resting at the top tells you to get set. And when the ball starts dropping it is a go. Now you can set your stupid watch to 1PM exactly.

Why did they start dropping balls? Well Robert Wauchope gets thinking while stuck at sea in 1818. He wants to know the local time without going to land. And if you check out the link there's lots of drawings involved. He keeps writing the British navy. And in 1829, the British Admiralty starts dropping balls from a tower in Portsmouth. And they're realling hoping Robert will stop sending them letters about it.1 Turns out it’s a good idea and in 1833 we got balls in Greenwich.

(1) I took a little liberty with this last piece of history.
